For a **1-hour online concert**, the sweet spot is to prepare **10 to 12 songs**.
If you try to sing non-stop for an hour, you will need about 15 songs, but you will completely exhaust your voice, and your online audience will get bored. An online show needs room to breathe.
Here is exactly how to break down your 1-hour setlist and why 10–12 songs is the perfect number:
## The 1-Hour Time Breakdown
An online show is different from a live stage show. You need to talk to the camera, interact with the chat, and give yourself time to drink water.
 * **Music (Approx. 40–45 minutes):** 11 songs multiplied by an average song length of 3.5 to 4 minutes = **~40 minutes of pure singing**.
 * **Talking & Interaction (Approx. 15–20 minutes):** This is your "crowd work." You need time for:
   * An introduction welcoming people to the stream.
   * Reading out names from the live chat and thanking people for comments or donations.
   * Telling brief, 1-minute stories behind the songs you are about to sing.
   * A wrap-up and goodbye at the end.
## How to Structure Your 11-Song Setlist
To keep people from clicking away (remembering that short online attention span!), you want to arrange your songs like a wave, changing the energy every few tracks.
| Section | Song Count | What to Play | The Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Opener** | 1 Song | Energetic, familiar, and welcoming. | **The Hook:** Grab their attention immediately so they stay on the stream. |
| **The Setup** | 2 Songs | Steady, upbeat, comfortable tempos. | Build the vibe and say a quick "hello" to the chat between them. |
| **The Deep Dive** | 3 Songs | Mid-tempo or emotional ballads. | This is where you show off your vocal talent and tell a personal story. |
| **The Interactive Break** | *No Song* | *Pure Chat Interaction* | Stop singing for 3–4 minutes. Read comments, answer questions, and let your voice rest. |
| **The Energy Boost** | 2 Songs | Fun, well-known covers, or high-energy tracks. | Wake the audience back up and get them typing in the chat. |
| **The Finale** | 2 Songs | Your absolute best, biggest, or most popular songs. | Leave them with a massive impression. |
| **The "Just in Case"** | 1 Song | A backup song (Encore). | Keep one extra song ready in case you fly through your setlist too fast! |
## 3 Essential Tips for an Online Show
 1. **Don't Over-Talk:** While interacting is great, don't let the gaps between songs last longer than 2 minutes. If you talk too long, people will leave the stream.
 2. **Keep Visuals Changing:** Since the camera is static, change the vibe using your lighting. If you sing a sad ballad, dim the lights. If you sing an upbeat song, make it bright.
 3. **Protect Your Voice:** Make sure you have a warm drink (like warm water with honey) next to you. Singing for 45 minutes straight on a live stream while trying to read a computer screen can dry your throat out faster than a normal stage performance.
